Methodology

Three lenses on urban sustainability

This atlas analyses the evolution of urban form and sustainability across more than 9,000 Functional Urban Areas (FUAs) worldwide between 2000 and 2015 — the full extent of the Global Human Settlement Layer.

Each FUA is characterised by three indicators that capture different dimensions of urban sustainability: ecological quality, land efficiency, and mobility access. Cities are classified as improving, stable or declining on each indicator, and clustered by their combined trajectory.

All maps are interactive: click, zoom, and hover over any dot to see the underlying data for that urban area.

Data Sources

Built on open data

GHSL
Global Human Settlement Layer — European Commission JRC. Built-up area, population grids at 100 m resolution.
GTFS
Compiled global public transport schedules. Stop-level frequency aggregated to FUA catchment areas.
MODIS
Terra MODIS NDVI time series (MOD13A3). Monthly composites aggregated to annual urban population-weighted means.
