About

This atlas analyses the evolution of urban form and sustainability across more than 9,000 Functional Urban Areas (FUAs) worldwide between 2000 and 2015, using the Global Human Settlement Layer as the primary data source.

Each FUA is characterised by three indicators capturing different dimensions of urban sustainability. Cities are classified as improving, stable, or declining on each indicator and grouped by their combined trajectory.

NDVI Share of urban population living in areas with >25% vegetation cover (NDVI > 0.25)
BUA / capita Built-up area per capita — a decrease signals more compact, efficient urban development
PT access Potential access to public transport stops with >3,000 annual departures within walkable distance

Data

GHSL Global Human Settlement Layer — European Commission JRC. Built-up area and population grids at 100 m resolution.
GTFS Compiled global public transport schedules. Stop-level frequency aggregated to FUA catchment areas.
MODIS Terra MODIS NDVI time series (MOD13A3). Monthly composites aggregated to annual population-weighted means.
OECD Functional Urban Areas delineation. City boundaries used as the spatial unit of analysis throughout.
